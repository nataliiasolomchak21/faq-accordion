FAQs

What is cloud computing?

Cloud computing refers to the delivery of computing services—including servers, storage, databases, networking, software, and more—over the internet (the cloud) on a pay-as-you-go basis. It offers flexibility, scalability, and cost-effectiveness compared to traditional on-premises infrastructure.

How does cloud computing enhance collaboration?

Cloud computing allows teams to access and collaborate on documents, files, and projects from anywhere with an internet connection. This promotes real-time collaboration, version control, and seamless communication among team members, regardless of their location.

Key benefits of cloud storage services?

Cloud storage services offer several advantages, including unlimited scalability, data redundancy, and accessibility from any device. Users can store, retrieve, and share files securely without the need for physical storage devices, reducing the risk of data loss and simplifying data management.

How does cloud computing improve disaster recovery?

Cloud computing provides robust disaster recovery solutions by replicating data across multiple geographic locations and ensuring high availability of services. In the event of a disaster or system failure, organizations can quickly restore operations and minimize downtime by accessing backup data stored in the cloud.
